The Problem: Scarcity of Clean Water



Lack of Knowledge

435 Million
People collect untreated surface water from lakes, ponds, rivers, and springs.

144 Million
People collecting untreated surface water from lakes, ponds, rivers and streams.

3.6 million people die every year from water-related diseases a year. Although boiling water can kill bacteria and parasites, preventing water-borne illnesses such as cholera, it cannot remove contaminants such as lead, oil, organic solvents, and pesticides â€” a fact that many do not yet understand. Even so, only 4.5% of 22 African countries boil water. This lack of knowledge means that many individuals assume their water is safe after boiling, when it can still contain many industrial contaminants.





Current Water Projects

50%
Of places with unsafe drinking water once had systems that functioned, but they fell apart due to lack of maintenance.

5%
Of water projects are visited at all after implementation.

Current water projects fail within 2-5 years, and less than 1% of these projects are monitored in the long term. NGOs usually have a group of villagers and expect them to operate and fix the systems without any support. The high maintenance involved means that users are unable to have clean water without a reliance on other people.





Industrial Wastewater

Only 8%
Of domestic and industrial wastewater is collected or treated in low-income countries.

842,000
Deaths in low & middle income countries were linked to contaminated water and inadequate sanitation.

Inadequate management of industrial and agricultural wastewater leads to dangerously polluted drinking water for millions of people. Compared to high-income countries, many low or middle-income countries lack the necessary resources to treat industrial and agricultural wastewater.
